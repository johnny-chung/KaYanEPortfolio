    Section 1

    The play episode…

    EN, you have been engaging in dramatic play in a variety of ways by using different kinds of play materials. When I put a basket of Legos and a box of paper figures of Disney characters on the carpet, you and your friends came and chose your favourite characters. You put Elsa figure into the house that your friend built. At the same time, you gave me Cinderella figure inviting me to play together. Therefore, we moved Elsa and Cinderella turning around inside the house. When asking you that “What is Elsa doing with Cinderella in the house?”, you smiled and replied, “They are dancing”. After playing for a while, you seemed to have some new ideas and started to connect several blue Legos to form a rectangle shape base next to the house. You then put Ariel figure on top and made it lay down, saying, “I build a pool”. I was curious about why you built this and asked, “What is Ariel doing here? Is she swimming?”. You smiled happily and replied, “No. Ariel is resting in the pool”.

    EN, it was realized that you really loved to play with Legos and figures during free choice time. One day, you came to the carpet area and started to build something with Legos again. You seemed to have a plan in mind and built a house with shelter for horse to take rest and kitchen area where some furniture and tools were placed. You used a person figure to move a plate with some food in front of a cat figure and shared with me excitingly, “Look! Kitten is eating snacks!”. Then you moved the person figure holding the plate to the kitchen area, saying with happy smile, “Kitten finished snacks. It’s time for doing dishes”.

    As you and your friends have spent a long time playing with figures and building scenes for characters to do different things, I think you may be interested in acting the roles of characters you like. Therefore, I created a collection of props like crowns, hats, masks and tools which represented different kinds of characters from cartoon or movies, and various jobs from daily life, and placed them in drama center. When you and your friends saw these props, you all looked amazed. After putting the crown onto your head, you pointed the wand towards your friend and wave it several times, saying that “You are frozen now!”. Your friend who had put on Captain American mask moved the shield forward pretending to block your magic. You both laughed and continued to act out different actions.

    EN, even when staying outside where not many toys were provided, you and your friend could still think of some creative ideas to have fun. Your friend sat in front of something which looked like two round chairs built by some other children previously, saying that “I’m on the beach now”. When you heard that your friend invited you to join on the beach, you replied loudly, “Yeah sure” and came to sit in front of another “chair”. You sat for a while and talked with your friend, “Would you like to be the king or queen? I want to be the king”. Your friend replied, “Then I’m the queen”. You then pretended to put something over your head and held something in your hand, saying that “King has a crown and a wand. I’m protecting the people”. You and your friend laughed and kept on acting out things that king and queen would do.

    What does it mean?

    EN, you have been interested and engaged in block play and dramatic play by planning what to build, generating innovative ideas how to play and acting out ideas with great enthusiasm, creativity and imagination (HDLH: Engagement - Body, Mind, and Senses). Your active engagement, long attention and strong emotional response of smiling and laughing show that you are keen on the play and enjoy so much fun from your favourite activities. You have developed representation skill (CoD # 4.3) as you used play materials of Legos to build and create scenes of dramatic play and added figures into the scenes as imaginative props to express ideas of what the characters would do in the house. You have also learnt to extend dramatic play into socio-dramatic by taking a role in play, co-operating and negotiating roles with your friends, and using language and imaginative props to act out ideas.

    Moreover, your happiness and excitement as demonstrated by laughing and interactions with peers show that you have also built up a sense of belonging and authentic and responsive relationship with others when making connections and interacting with friends and teachers in play (HDLH: Belonging). You have developed social skill of cooperating (CoD # 1.6) as you were willing to participate in play by taking and negotiating roles, listening and responding to peers positively, communicating ideas and sharing materials during play. You have developed social skill of interacting with adults (CoD # 1.9) when engaging and interacting with adults in play in positive ways.

    In addition, you are a capable communicator who can express your ideas and thoughts and communicate with your friends, teachers and families well in many different ways (HDLH: Expression). You have developed skill of using verbal and non-verbal communication (CoD # 3.1) as you could express your ideas, respond to others, and describe the scenes, events, feelings or actions when manipulating figures or pretending to be someone to do or say something in various ways such as gestures, facial expression and tones with increasingly complex sentences.

    What are the possibilities?

    EN, your interest in and creative ideas for developing dramatic play could be connected to your life experience of seeing families doing different things such as cooking, doing dishes and feeding pets or the experience of watching cartoons and reading books about living place and lives of characters.

    In response to sustaining and extending your interest and skill, apart from continuing to observe and document your play, I will share and discuss with your family to help me know more about your life experience and suggest what other materials can be brought into your play associating with your experience and interest. I will also arrange several field trips, for example, visiting vet clinic or adoption center where you and your friends can learn to how to take care of pets, going to parks where you can observe what other families and children do while enjoying outdoor play and, visiting grocery stores or café where you can learn and observe the process of buying groceries, preparing food and serving customers, or visiting musical theatre where you can watch performance that combines songs, spoken dialogue, acting and dance.

    After conducting field trips, I will discuss with you and your peers about your experience, thoughts and feeling in circle time. I will also arrange the environment with different art, writing and play materials for you to think, revisit, rebuild and represent what you have learnt and experienced during field trips through various kinds of creations like drawings, models, and especially dramatic play. Different kinds of block materials including Legos, hollow blocks and building blocks in different size and shape will be provided motivating you to develop and implement your plans of creating scenes of dramatic play. Also, a variety of play materials with division of different themes will be placed in drama center, for instance, clothing and accessories for dress-up, pretend cooking tools and food sets, stuffed animal toys or baby dolls, tools and equipment related to different themes and jobs like being doctor, nurse, cooks or waiters.

    EN, I will also place some art and writing materials like playdough, air-dry clay, cardboard, coloured paper, crayons, markers and glue in art center. I think you might be interested in creating props and signs and adding some interesting and meaningful elements that you observe in daily life or from field trips into your dramatic play. For example, you can cooperate with your peers to make some menu or recipe for your café, or make labels and signs to decorate drama area and turn it into a vet clinic. What you have made can eventually be used as props to act out scenarios when taking certain roles in dramatic play. For example, you can pretend to be a customer reading the menu you make whereas your friend who dress in waiter uniform serve you in a café.

    My Reflection:

    When looking at the photos and thinking about the observations, I noticed that EN was actively involved in dramatic play and developing concepts of representation. I shared it with my student educator friend. We talked about EN, and it was amazing to realize that she has vivid imagination and creativity and can use different ways like using imaginative props and language to express ideas and act out the scenarios. It is suggested I can share my observations with your family to discuss what you have experienced or love to do in leisure time. Your home experience and culture can be brought into the classroom and incorporated into your dramatic play which helps enhance greater interest to get involved in play and reinforce your learning and development of social, cognition and language skills. I wonder how activities can be organized and what other kinds of materials can be added to enrich your experience, play and learning.

    Section 2

    How the learning story about EN’s dramatic play is assessment

    Learning stories can be viewed as important assessment as it is pedagogical documentation involving the “process of gathering and analyzing evidence of learning” that “make thinking and learning visible” and “provides the foundation for assessment for, as , and of learning” (Hunter, 2023, p. 18). To collect information about children’s learning for future analysis, interpretation and discussion, it is required to observe, listen and record children and their play experience, learning process and products in different ways (Hunter, 2023). With inclusion of photos and summary of notes made for recording and capturing individual and group play experience taken place both indoors and outdoors, my learning story about EN has recorded, described and celebrated her learning experience and process related to the interest in dramatic play based on my observations of her active engagement in favourite activities, her ideas, feelings and emotional response, and her interactions with different people and various kinds of play materials.

    Documentation of EN’s play becomes a visual record that makes her learning evident for understanding her thinking process, finding meaning of her actions and experiences, and evaluating her learning and development (OPE Print Package, 2018-2019). When looking into what EN has done and experienced, it is realized that through taking on roles and acting them out, EN use dramatic play to explore and express own thoughts and feelings and experience lives and surroundings. Regarding her learning and development, it is found that she is developing and practicing skills of representation, communication and cooperation when engaging in dramatic play and interacting with people and materials. All these examinations of interests, strengths, skills and development provide basis for program planning that is child-centered and play-based, exploration of possibilities and inquiries, reflection and modification of teaching practice, and discussion with children, families, and staff team (Hunter, 2023).

    In the learning story, through ongoing reflections and discussions with staff teams and families, I have explored a number of possibilities which can be incorporated into program plans and more responsive to EN’s needs, interests and skills. For instance, various activities including fieldtrips and follow-up discussions with arrangement of play areas, provision of different kinds of play materials and inclusion of home culture and experience will be planned and implemented to create an inclusive and responsive environment with interactive experience. Moreover, EN’s learning story shared with families and displayed in center allows families and visitors to know more about the child’s playing experience and learning condition in the program and provide feedback as it serves as a documentation that make what children do, think and learn and how the program takes this important aspect into consideration become visible and evident for families and visitors to the center (OPE Print Package, 2018-2019).

    The way of making children’s learning and understanding visible can invite communication and contribution of different parties including children, families, educators and communities that help facilitate children’s growth and development and strengthen connections with families and communities.
