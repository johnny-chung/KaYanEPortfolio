    Section 1

    Self Assessment:

    1.

    As a student educator who is going to enter the field of early childhood education, I would think of my role in my practice as co-learners with others including children, families and other educators. I would engage in developing mutually respectful and responsive relationships with children, families and other educators, gain more understanding about them and learn together with them, share my learning and experience and learn about their knowledge and perspectives (Ontario Ministry of Education, 2014b)/ Through observations, I have documented and assessed children’s play, and planed activities responding to their interests and skills they are developing. After implementing activities, I would reflect on what worked and what I could improve next time, and seek out information from multiple perspectives by sharing my observations and reflections with children, families and staff team. When asking feedbacks from children, it helps me learn more about their needs and interests, creative thinking and innovative ideas which are conducive for planning activities and enriching play. At the same time, children would learn that their opinions are valued and be more engaged in play where they have contributed their ideas. When discussing my observation and reflection with staff team, I learn more about how to adjust my strategies and the activities so support and promote full participation of children while meeting their individual needs based on their developmental level. In turn, my colleagues can also have better understanding of children’s interests, strengths and needs which is important for planning and implementing curriculum. When sharing my observation about children’s play with their families, it helps me know more about what children love to do at home and think of ways to connect such interests when designing next activities. In turn, families would learn more about what their children have done and experienced in the centre and find ways to support and extend their play after school.

    2.

    Based on my practice and the notes above, it is realized that additional knowledge about various kinds of strategies to accommodate for different needs and condition of children with flexibility when planning and implementing activities and helping them to regulate emotions and behaviour. In my future career path, I will continuously learn to maintain positive communication with children to understand their needs and interests through listening to what they talk about patiently and observing how they explore and interact with different people and materials things with respect for their choice. It is needed to keep on reflecting my practice, share and discuss my observation and reflection with the staff team and families to learn about how they develop strategies to respond to interests, needs and developmental skills of individuals and groups of children during play, to meet children’s individual needs, and to respond to children who need extra support, and how they interact with children to help them regulate their emotions and behaviour. Besides, I will attend courses and communicate with other professionals who are experts of supporting children with special needs to learn about how to provide support and guidance in response to their needs appropriately while facilitating their growth and development.

    3.

    The specific area for growth that I need to continue to work on is to improve and individualize strategies to promote positive interactions that respond to children’s needs and to shows flexibility in order to respond to individual needs of children while supporting and enhancing growth and development of the whole group of children. This connects to the highlights of College of ECE in the Code of Ethics and Standards of Practice (2017) that it is required for educators to “Respond to the uniqueness of individuals and groups of children. They identify appropriate strategies, access the necessary resources and design curriculum to ensure full participation of all children, considering ability, cultural and linguistic diversity, and Indigenous identity. They provide all children with opportunities for engagement, exploration, and expression (Standard II C, 4).
